Operations

Operational Model (Non-technical)

Our work follows a humanitarian “information cycle” focused on needs, not surveillance. We collect and validate reports to reduce harm and deliver assistance efficiently.

  • Assess: listen to community requests; map needs and risks.
  • Verify: cross-check with local partners; avoid rumor propagation.
  • Coordinate: match needs with capacity; schedule distribution and follow-up.
  • Document: record only what is necessary; protect identities by default.
  • Review: continuous safeguarding and ethics checks.

Active Lines of Service

Representative focus areas commonly requested across the region.

  • Education Access: mobile lessons, literacy support, materials distribution.
  • Health Support: clinic supply routing, translation, appointment coordination.
  • Family Relief: food parcels, winter kits, emergency referrals.
  • Legal Navigation: trusted referrals for documentation and safe travel guidance.
  • Safeguarding: child protection, abuse reporting routes, volunteer vetting.
